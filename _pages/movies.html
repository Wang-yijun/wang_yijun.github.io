Watch my subduction-model playlist for EGU 2025 on YouTube

This is a collection of movies created for Wang et al. (2025). The videos compare two 3-D subduction models side by side: one with a retreating trench and one with a stationary trench. The goal is to illustrate how these two settings generate different mantle-flow patterns and how those differences influence olivine texture evolution and anisotropic viscosity.

Movie 1 — Cross section (particles placed on the cross section; poloidal flow): olivine texture evolution and anisotropy

This movie shows a cross section through the center of the subduction zone. Viscosity is displayed as the background color, and the 3-D olivine texture orientation is overlaid as line segments. Orientations that are out of the plane appear as points, while in-plane orientations appear as lines at full length. Line color represents the strength of the olivine texture (and associated anisotropy): pink indicates a weak, dispersed texture, while yellow indicates a strong, well-aligned texture.

Movie 2 — Cross section (particles placed on the cross section; poloidal flow): cluster assignment

In Wang et al. (2025), we use time-series clustering to identify recurring patterns in olivine texture evolution and anisotropic viscosity in subduction zones. The input consists of time-series data for deformation, texture, and anisotropic viscosity; the output is a set of clusters based on the similarity of their temporal evolution. Particles within the same cluster therefore share similar histories of deformation, texture development, and anisotropic viscosity. In this movie, particles are colored by their cluster assignment to highlight regions with similar evolution histories.
